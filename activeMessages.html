FOR THE ATTENTION OF ALL LEVEL 1 AND LEVEL 2 USERS: If you receive an unexpected email, telephone call or letter from someone who claims to be from the Home Office, it may be a scam. In particular, the Home Office will never contact you to ask you for your SMS user ID or password or to provide you with a password with which to log into SMS. SMS users must not give their user ID or password to anyone else. If you forget your SMS password you must use the Forgotten your password? link in SMS to reset it. If you are contacted by the Home Office and you have any concerns you can call us on 0300 123 4699. You can also contact us by e-mail: work sponsors should contact the Business Helpdesk at BusinessHelpdesk@homeoffice.gov.uk and student sponsors the Educators Helpdesk at EducatorsHelpdesk@homeoffice.gov.uk. Please also see Fraud, tricks and scams: guidance - GOV.UK (www.gov.uk) for further advice and guidance. 15/05/2024
All work sponsors: CoS assigned prior to 4 April 2024** We are aware that workers with a CoS assigned prior to 4 April 2024 who make their visa application after this date are unable to select a standard occupation code (SOC) from the SOC2010 list. Where this happens, you should provide your worker with the relevant new SOC2020 code. You can use the CASCOT occupational coding tool available at CascotWeb (warwick.ac.uk) to do this. WORKERS APPLYING FOR A SKILLED WORKER VISA whose job was on the shortage occupation list should also select the option on the application form for a job on the immigration salary list to ensure they are asked to pay the lower application fee. 01/05/2024

FOR THE ATTENTION OF SKILLED WORK SPONSORS: IMMIGRATION SKILLS CHARGE (ISC) FOR WORKERS SWITCHING FROM THE GRADUATE ROUTE. You are reminded that you are not exempt from paying the ISC if you wish to sponsor a worker whose permission is as a Graduate or as a dependant of someone on the study routes listed in paragraph S5.11 of Part 2 of the Workers and Temporary Workers Guidance for Sponsors. You are also reminded that when assigning CoS for these workers you must use the Skilled Worker (Switching immigration category - ISC liable) CoS type. You must not use the Skilled Worker (graduate in Tier 4/Student route switching to Skilled Worker) COS type. Failure to assign the correct COS type and pay the appropriate ISC fee will result in a delay to your worker`s visa or leave application consideration and may result in the application being refused. 01/05/2024
For the attention of Work Sponsors-Updated sponsor guidance: 04/04/24**The following sponsor guidance documents have been updated to reflect important changes to the Immigration Rules coming into effect on 4 April 2024: Part 2: Sponsor a worker, Sponsor a Skilled Worker, Sponsor a GBM worker, Sponsor a MOR/Religious Worker, Sponsor an International Sportsperson, Sponsor a GAE worker, Sponsor a Scale-up Worker, Sponsor a Seasonal Worker. The changes include increased salary thresholds for most of these routes and changes to eligible occupation codes. Some other minor clarifications have also been made. See ?changes to this guidance? in the relevant document for details. The revised guidance will be available from 9 am on 4 April at: www.gov.uk/government/collections/sponsorship-information-for-employers-and-educators#workers-and-temporary-workers:-guidance-for-sponsors 03/04/2024
